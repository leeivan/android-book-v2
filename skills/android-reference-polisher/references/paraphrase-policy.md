# Paraphrase Policy

This skill exists to improve chapters with reference support, not to reproduce source text.

## Non-negotiable rules

- Do not quote source paragraphs.
- Do not translate source paragraphs into Chinese.
- Do not preserve source sentence order if you can avoid it.
- Do not lift examples wholesale and only rename variables.
- Do not mirror a source chapter's section order unless it independently matches the repo's teaching flow.

## What to extract instead

Extract only these kinds of notes from a reference:

- the reader problem the section is solving
- the mental model behind the topic
- useful contrasts or tradeoffs
- example types worth recreating
- pitfalls worth warning about
- practice ideas worth adapting

## Safe rewrite pattern

1. Read the source passage.
2. Close it mentally and summarize the idea in your own short notes.
3. Reopen only to verify facts or terminology.
4. Write new chapter prose from the notes, not from the source sentences.

## Red flags

Rewrite again if you notice any of these:

- the paragraph sounds like translated English
- the sentence rhythm or example ordering clearly mirrors one source
- the wording is much more polished than your surrounding chapter voice
- a chapter subsection could be reverse-mapped one-to-one to a source subsection

## Recommended synthesis pattern

- combine the repo's existing chapter goal
- combine one or two reference-book ideas
- combine the repo's style guide and full-prose standard
- produce one fresh chapter section in the repo's own teaching voice
